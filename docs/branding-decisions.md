# Vibe Radar — Branding Decisions

_Last updated: 2026-04-01_

---

## Name

**Decision: Keep "Vibe Radar" for now. Revisit at public launch.**

- "Vibe" is trend-dependent and slightly generic
- If renaming before launch: preferred alternative is **Frequenz** (European underground credibility, ownable, ages well)
- If staying with Vibe Radar: compensate in every other touchpoint — visual, copy, and tone must do the heavy lifting

---

## Tagline

**Decision: "Your taste. Tonight."**

- No alternatives. This is locked.
- Use as: landing page hero, key app screen, App Store subtitle, pitch opener

---

## Positioning

**Decision: Scene-credibility play, not mass-market utility.**

- Target: serious electronic music fans who treat their taste as identity
- Competitive frame: "RA shows you everything. Vibe Radar shows you yours."
- Do NOT compete on comprehensiveness — curate hard, surface only high-confidence matches
- Foil is Resident Advisor (too broad), not Bandsintown (wrong genre/audience)

---

## Audience Persona — "Alex"

- Age 27, Madrid (Malasaña/Lavapiés), UX designer or digital creative
- Listens to melodic techno, afro house, organic house, minimal
- Goes out 2–3×/month, decides same-day Friday/Saturday
- Current discovery: Instagram (unreliable) → RA (overwhelming) → word of mouth
- Core pain: finds out about the right events after they sell out
- Will pay €4.99/month if the value is immediately obvious on first use

---

## Tone of Voice

**Three rules:**

1. **Insider, not gatekeeper** — write like the friend who knows every promoter, not an institution
2. **Precise, not verbose** — if it can be two words, make it two words
3. **Understated cool** — don't hype, don't exclaim; let the event speak

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "3 matches for you tonight in Lavapiés." | "We found amazing events just for you!" |
| "92% taste match. Doors at midnight." | "You might love this based on your listening!" |
| "Sold out. You needed this earlier." | "Oops! This event is no longer available." |

---

## Colour Palette

- Background: `#0a0815`
- Cyan: `#00f0ff`
- Magenta: `#ff00aa`

These are confirmed. Dark, underground, technical. Do not add warm tones or lighten the palette for "accessibility" without careful consideration.

---

## Growth Mechanic to Build First

**Decision: Shareable taste profile (Wrapped-style)**

Before optimising the matching algorithm further, ship a shareable profile screen:

> *Your sound: melodic techno / afro house / organic house*
> *94% taste match → Maho Sawa at Sala Equis, Saturday*

This is the primary acquisition tool. Electronic music fans share identity signals. Make it screenshot-worthy.

---

## What Not to Do

- No comprehensive event indexing — curate, don't aggregate
- No hype copy, exclamation marks, or promoter-style language
- No broad genre expansion early — own Madrid electronic scene first, then Barcelona, then Berlin
- No warm/friendly onboarding tone — assume knowledge, respect the user's depth
